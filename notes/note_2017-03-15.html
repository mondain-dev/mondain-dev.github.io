Neven Sesardić, “From Bad Logic to Bad Philosophy: the Case of Alain Badiou,” Rightly Considered, (10 March, 2017, archived):

\[\begin{align} (\exists x)(Sx & \rightarrow \neg Sa), \label{ref1} \\ \forall xSx & \rightarrow \neg Sa, \label{ref2} \end{align}\]

and \((\ref{ref1}) \leftrightarrow (\ref{ref2})\).

Proof: To show that \((\ref{ref1}) \rightarrow (\ref{ref2})\), we show that \(\{(\ref{ref1}), \forall xSx \}\vdash \neg Sa \), then by deduction theorem we have \((\ref{ref1}) \rightarrow (\ref{ref2})\).

\[\begin{align} (\exists x)(Sx \rightarrow \neg Sa) &\ \ \ \ &\text{(assumption)} \nonumber \\ Sb \rightarrow \neg Sa & & \text{(existential elimination)} \label{ref4}\\ \forall xSx & & \text{(assumption)} \nonumber \\ Sb & & \text{(universal elimination)} \label{ref6} \\ \neg Sa & & \text{(\ref{ref4}, \ref{ref6}, modus ponens)} \nonumber \end{align}\]

\((\ref{ref2}) \rightarrow (\ref{ref1})\):

\[\begin{align} \forall xSx \rightarrow \neg Sa &\ \ \ \ & \text{(assumption)} \nonumber \\ Sa \rightarrow \neg\forall xSx & & \text{(modus tollens)} \nonumber \\ Sa \rightarrow \exists x \neg Sx & & \text{(negation of } \forall\text{)} \label{ref10} \\ \exists x \neg Sx \rightarrow \neg Sb & & \text{(existential elimination)} \label{ref11} \\ Sa \rightarrow \neg Sb & & \text{(\ref{ref10}, \ref{ref11}, hypothetical syllogism)} \nonumber \\ Sb \rightarrow \neg Sa & & \text{(modus tollens)} \nonumber \\ (\exists x)(Sx \rightarrow \neg Sa) & & \text{(existential introduction)} \nonumber \end{align}\]

Hence, \((\ref{ref1}) \leftrightarrow (\ref{ref2})\). \(\blacksquare\)

Remark: More generally, we can derive from \(\{\neg \forall xSx\}\) that

\[\begin{aligned} \neg \forall xSx &\ \ \ \ & \text{(assumption)} \\ \exists x \neg Sx & & \text{(negation of } \forall\text{)} \\ \neg Sb & & \text{(existential elimination)} \\ \neg Sb \rightarrow (\neg Pc \rightarrow \neg Sb) & & \text{(logical axiom)} \\ \neg Pc \rightarrow \neg Sb & & \text{(modus ponens)} \\ Sb \rightarrow Pc & & \text{(modus tollens)} \\ (\exists x)(Sx \rightarrow Pc) & & \text{(existential introduction)} \\ (\forall X \forall y )(\exists x)(Sx \rightarrow Xy) & & \text{(universal introduction)} \\ \end{aligned}\]

Since \((\ref{ref1}) \leftrightarrow (\ref{ref2}) \leftrightarrow \neg \forall xSx\) (see Sesardić), we have \((\ref{ref1}) \rightarrow (\forall X \forall y )(\exists x)(Sx \rightarrow Xy)\). On the other hand, \((\ref{ref1})\) is an instance of \((\forall X \forall y )(\exists x)(Sx \rightarrow Xy)\) with \(X=\neg S\) and \(y=a\), and therefore \( (\ref{ref1}) \leftrightarrow (\forall X \forall y)(\exists x)(Sx \rightarrow Xy) \), showing that the second \(S\) can be generalized, and the constant \(a\) is immaterial, contrary to Badiou’s claim that ‘the predicate \(S\) is altogether particular, and is not replaceable by any predicate whatsoever, no more than is the individual constant \(a\)’.
